Based on an audit of how approved leaves interact with the rest of the application, there are **three key areas** that should be updated to make the system 100% robust and consistent:

---

### 1. The Admin Dashboard Stats (Most Critical) ⚠️
*   **The Issue:** On the web/desktop admin dashboard, the system counts **who is absent today**. Currently, if an employee has an approved leave, doesn't clock in, and today is a normal working day, the backend groups them under **`absentEmployees`** and adds them to the **`absentToday`** count.
*   **Why it's bad:** HR managers will see employees flagged as "Absent" when they are actually away on approved leave.
*   **The Fix:** Update `getDashboardStats` in `attendance.service.ts` to check for approved leaves today. We should:
    1.  Filter leave-takers out of the `absentEmployees` list.
    2.  Add a new category: **`onLeaveToday`** and return **`onLeaveEmployees`** so the admin dashboard can show exactly who is out on leave.

---

### 2. PDF Attendance Export Styling (Visual Polish) 🎨
*   **The Issue:** When generating monthly or term PDF reports, the daily log correctly lists the status as `LEAVE (Sick Leave)` or `LEAVE (Annual Leave)` (which is great!). However, the PDF styling defaults the text color to standard black.
*   **The Fix:** Update `AttendanceExportService` to render the `LEAVE` status in a professional, distinct color (like **teal** or **blue**) so managers can visually scan monthly logs and easily tell leaves apart from active work (green) and absences (red).

---

### 3. Next Shift Planning & Scheduling Guards (Logic Safety) 📅
*   **The Issue:** The dashboard computes a countdown or lists when the employee's "Next Shift" is scheduled. If their next typical shift falls on a day they have approved leave for, the system shouldn't recommend it or count down to it.
*   **The Fix:** When finding the "Next Shift" date (e.g. for the countdown banner), make sure the calculation skips dates that fall within the employee's approved leaves.
