Your Leave Management system currently works beautifully, has a robust flow (Requests $\rightarrow$ Approvals $\rightarrow$ FCM Push Notifications $\rightarrow$ Calendar Blocks $\rightarrow$ PDF Color Coding), and completely solves your core attendance business logic. 

However, if you want to elevate it to **professional enterprise SaaS / industry standards** (similar to systems like *BambooHR*, *Workday*, or *Freshteam*), there are **four main areas** where it could be improved:

---

### 1. Strict Overlap Prevention (High Priority) 🛡️
*   **The Industry Standard:** An employee should never be allowed to submit a leave request for dates that overlap with an *already existing* pending or approved leave request. (e.g., requesting Casual Leave for May 15–20 when they already have approved Sick Leave for May 17–19).
*   **Current State:** Right now, there is no validation blocking overlapping dates. An employee could theoretically request overlapping leaves, causing report duplication.
*   **The Improvement:** Add a database query check during the request stage to block submission if the requested date range clashes with an existing pending/approved leave for that employee.

---

### 2. Date Flow Sanity Checks (Medium Priority) 📅
*   **The Industry Standard:** Ensure all date ranges are mathematically logical.
*   **Current State:** The backend currently accepts the dates as strings directly. There are no safeguards ensuring:
    *   The `startDate` is not in the past (retroactive requests).
    *   The `startDate` is before or equal to the `endDate` (e.g., preventing a start date of May 25th with an end date of May 15th).
*   **The Improvement:** Implement validation to ensure `endDate >= startDate`, and optionally require that leaves are requested at least 1–2 days in advance (except for emergency types like `SICK` leave).

---

### 3. Leave Balances / Entitlements (Premium Enterprise Feature) 💼
*   **The Industry Standard:** Organizations assign employees a fixed number of leave days per calendar year (e.g., 21 days of Annual Leave, 10 days of Sick Leave, 5 days of Casual Leave). Every time a leave is approved, it deducts from their balance, and the system blocks them if they exceed their quota.
*   **Current State:** Currently, there are no quotas or tracking of balances. Employees can apply for unlimited leaves, and it relies entirely on the HR Admin's memory to reject requests that exceed policies.
*   **The Improvement:** Create a `LeaveBalance` entity that tracks remaining days for each leave category and deducts days automatically upon approval.

---

### 4. Supporting Attachments / Medical Certificates (Enterprise Standard) 🩺
*   **The Industry Standard:** If an employee applies for `SICK` leave for more than 2 consecutive days, they are usually required to upload a doctor's note or medical certificate.
*   **Current State:** The mobile and web forms only support text reasons.
*   **The Improvement:** Add an optional `attachmentUrl` field in the database schema to allow employees to upload images or PDF documents directly from the mobile app.

---
