To design a system capable of managing **2,000+ active employees** storing **10+ years of operational data** without breaking, we have to transition from a "Minimum Viable Product" to a **highly scalable, high-concurrency enterprise architecture**.

At this scale, a single unpaginated API request like `fetch('/leaves')` or rendering thousands of card elements synchronously will completely crash the browser, freeze the user interface, and overload your backend database.

Here is my **3-Tier Enterprise Scaling Plan** to handle millions of rows and thousands of daily active users seamlessly:

---

### 1. Data Projections (The Scale Analysis)
Let's look at the mathematical reality of your data over 10 years:
*   **Leave Requests:** $2,000\text{ employees} \times 4\text{ requests/year} \times 10\text{ years} \approx \mathbf{80,000\text{ rows}}$. (Highly manageable).
*   **Attendance Logs:** $2,000\text{ employees} \times 250\text{ workdays} \times 2\text{ clock events} \times 10\text{ years} \approx \mathbf{10,000,000\text{ (10 Million) rows}}$. (Critical scaling bottleneck).

---

### 2. Tier 1: Backend & Database Scale-Up (NestJS + Postgres)

*   **Database Indexing (High Priority):** 
    We must add composite indices on `(employee_id, start_date, status)` for leaves, and `(employee_id, timestamp)` for attendance logs. This ensures query retrieval remains under **10 milliseconds** even with 10 million rows.
*   **Table Partitioning:** 
    We will partition the massive `attendance_log` table by **Year** (e.g., `attendance_log_2026`, `attendance_log_2027`). When a manager runs a report for this month, PostgreSQL will query *only* the current year's light partition, completely ignoring the other 9 years of historical data.
*   **Cold-Storage Archiving:** 
    Create a scheduled background job to archive logs older than 5 years into compressed read-only cold storage, keeping the active operational tables incredibly fast and light.

---

### 3. Tier 2: API Gateway Scale-Up (NestJS)

*   **Server-Side Pagination:** 
    We will rewrite the `/leaves` endpoints to require pagination parameters: `GET /api/v1/leaves?page=1&limit=15`. The server will only fetch 15 records at a time, accompanied by a metadata block (e.g., `totalItems: 80000`, `totalPages: 5333`).
*   **Server-Side Filtering:** 
    Instead of loading all leaves and letting the client search, we implement server-side filters: `GET /api/v1/leaves?search=Kwakutheo&branchId=2&startDate=2026-01-01`.

---

### 4. Tier 3: Frontend Modernization (Next.js Dashboard)

*   **Paginated Table / Infinite Scroll:** 
    Replace the infinite vertical list with a paginated, tabular data grid layout.
*   **Server-Side Search & Filter Bar:**
    We will add a beautiful, responsive search panel at the top of the Leave Requests page:
    1.  **Search Input:** Search by Employee Name or Code (triggers debounced server requests).
    2.  **Branch & Department Filters:** Selectors to filter by branch/dept.
    3.  **Date-Range Picker:** Allows looking up a specific month or year of historical leaves instead of downloading a decade of data.
*   **DOM Virtualization:** 
    If a page ever displays more than 100 rows, we will use virtualization (rendering only the rows currently visible inside the scroll container), keeping browser RAM footprint practically at zero.
